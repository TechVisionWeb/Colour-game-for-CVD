  • Attempt this experiment in a Chrome (colour blind simulated) or Edge - typical colour vision

Transition task

An example of the task is show below. For this task you must drag the middle (2-9) blocks and place the blocks of colour in the correct gradient order. The middle (2-9) blocks are compared to the first and last blocks. The more a colour contains black, the closer its order placement is to black. The more a colour contains white the closer in order placement it is to white. To drag in the experiment, click the square, and keep the mouse button pressed until placed in the desired space, then let go. You will be notified of the time left above the transition blocks. I recommend you practice this action with the transition below. If there are problems with this action email me.

Sorting task

A square block with the same dimensions as the transition block ,is coloured a specific colour. This colour must be dragged and placed into a square that is larger and labelled as the specific colour. To drag during the experiment, click the square, and keep the mouse button pressed until placed in the desired larger square, then let go. The smaller square should revert back to its original starting position and you will be notified of your chosen colour and time left just above the larger square blocks.

CVD individual-only information

Colour Iconizer for the sorting tasks

The label above tells you what colour the symbol represents. The objects used are associated with that colour. These symbols will be present in an aided sorting task. They will be used to identify what the colour of the hue square is, for example, a yellow square will have a star symbol underneath it.

Take your time to memorise what each symbol represents.

Colour Mix for transition tasks

The label above tells you what colour the symbol represents. The objects used are associated with that colour. These symbols will be present in the 3 aided transition tasks. They will be used to identify how much of a colour is contained in a square along a gradient. The transition blocks will need to be rearranged in the correct order just like in the unaided transition task. The difference is the coloured square will have a symbol underneath it. For example, a yellow square will have a lightning symbol underneath it.

Take your time to memorise what each symbol represents. There are situations where pink can be interpreted as a light red.

An example of how to use Colour Mix is shown below.THE STROKE LINE THICKNESS AND TRANSPARENCY IS RELATIVE TO THE TRANSITION LINE'S START AND END SYMBOLS. The more transparent a symbol is and the less thick the symbol stroke width is, the less of the colour is present in the mixture. The more opaque and the thicker the symbol stroke width is, the more the colour is present in the mixture. Purple, bluish green, yellowish green and orange are a mixture of two colours, combining their symbols.

  • Red + yellow = orange.
  • Green + blue = bluish green .
  • Blue + red = purple.
  • Yellow + green = yellowish green

When mixing a colour with grey, the greyer the colour, the more the drawing inside the symbol decreases in size. The more saturated or less grey there is in a colour, the larger the symbol. When a colour is completely grey, there is no symbol in the square.
